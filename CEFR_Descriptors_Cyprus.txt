HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT
When a teacher selects a school level and requests curriculum-aligned
activities, search this document for the relevant class entry. Use the
"Can do" descriptors to ensure tasks are pitched at the correct level.
Reference the class name and CEFR level in your output.

To locate a class: search for the class name in CAPS.
Example: "A GYMNASIUM" or "B LYCEUM" or "C TECHNICAL"

================================================================
CLASS: A GYMNASIUM | CEFR LEVEL: A2
================================================================

LISTENING
- Can follow speech that is very slow and carefully articulated with long pauses.
- Can understand phrases related to immediate priority areas (personal info, shopping, local geography, employment) when clearly articulated.
- Can catch the main point in short, clear, simple messages and announcements.
- Can extract essential information from short recorded passages on predictable everyday matters delivered slowly.
- Can follow the plot of a story told slowly and clearly.
- Can identify the main point of TV news items where visual supports commentary.

READING
- Can understand short, simple texts with highest frequency vocabulary.
- Can understand short simple personal emails and very simple formal letters.
- Can understand everyday signs and notices in public places and workplaces.
- Can locate specific information in lists.
- Can identify specific information in simpler written material (letters, brochures, short articles) especially with visual support.
- Can understand simple instructions on everyday equipment.

SPEAKING
- Can give a simple description of people, living/working conditions, daily routines, likes/dislikes as a short series of simple phrases.
- Can handle very short social exchanges; rarely able to keep conversation going independently.
- Can ask and answer simple questions; initiate and respond to simple statements in familiar areas.
- Can use simple polite forms of greeting and address.
- Can ask how people are and react to news.
- Can discuss what to do, where to go, make arrangements to meet.
- Can make and respond to suggestions; agree and disagree.
- Can exchange opinions and compare using simple language.
- Can describe him/herself, what he/she does, where he/she lives.
- Can describe people, places and possessions in simple terms.
- Can say what he/she likes and dislikes.
- Can present opinion in simple terms provided interlocutors are patient.
- Can give a short, rehearsed, basic presentation on a familiar subject.

WRITING
- Can write a series of simple phrases and sentences linked with simple connectors.
- Can write about family, living conditions, educational background in simple phrases.
- Can create short imaginary biographies and simple poems.
- Can create diary entries describing activities using basic vocabulary and simple connectives (and, but, because).
- Can compose an introduction to a story or continue a story with dictionary help.
- Can ask for or pass on personal details in written form.
- Can write very simple personal emails.
- Can write very simple personal letters expressing thanks and apology.
- Can write short, simple notes and messages relating to immediate needs.

MEDIATION
- Can use simple words to ask someone to explain something.
- Can recognise when difficulties occur and indicate the apparent nature of a problem.
- Can convey the main point(s) of short, simple conversations or texts on everyday subjects, provided these are expressed clearly.

LINGUISTIC COMPETENCE
- Vocabulary: Sufficient for basic communicative needs and simple survival needs.
- Grammar: Uses some simple structures correctly; still makes basic mistakes (mixes tenses, forgets agreement); it is usually clear what is meant.
- Vocabulary control: Narrow repertoire for concrete, everyday needs.
- Phonology: Very limited repertoire understood with effort by native speakers.
- Orthography: Can copy familiar words; writes with reasonable phonetic accuracy.
- Sociolinguistic: Can handle very short social exchanges; can make and respond to invitations, apologies.

CLASS: B GYMNASIUM | CEFR LEVEL: A2+
================================================================

LISTENING
- Can follow speech that is very slow and carefully articulated with long pauses.
- Can understand phrases related to immediate priority areas when clearly articulated.
- Can understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters.
- Can understand and follow a series of instructions for familiar activities (sports, cooking) if delivered slowly.
- Can understand straightforward announcements (cinema, sports events, train delays) if delivery is slow.
- Can extract essential information from short recorded passages on predictable everyday matters.
- Can follow the plot of a story told slowly and clearly.
- Can identify the main point of TV news items where visual supports commentary.

READING
- Can understand short, simple texts on familiar matters consisting of high frequency or job-related language.
- Can understand basic types of standard routine emails, letters on familiar topics.
- Can find specific, predictable information in simple everyday material (ads, menus, timetables).
- Can understand everyday signs and notices.
- Can identify specific information in simpler written material (letters, brochures, short newspaper articles).
- Can understand regulations expressed in simple language (e.g. safety rules).

SPEAKING
- Can give a simple description of people, living/working conditions, daily routines as a short series of simple phrases.
- Can interact with reasonable ease in structured situations and short conversations with support.
- Can manage simple routine exchanges without undue effort.
- Can enter unprepared into conversation on familiar topics and express personal opinions.
- Can establish social contact: greetings, farewells, introductions, thanks.
- Can express and respond to feelings (surprise, happiness, sadness, interest, indifference).
- Can generally follow main points in informal discussions with friends.
- Can give or seek personal views and opinions.
- Can narrate a story; give detailed accounts of experiences, describing feelings and reactions.
- Can explain likes/dislikes, making simple direct comparisons.
- Can give a short, rehearsed presentation on a topic, briefly give reasons and explanations; can cope with limited straightforward follow-up questions.

WRITING
- Can write a series of simple phrases and sentences linked with simple connectors.
- Can describe everyday aspects of their environment (people, places, job/study experience) in linked sentences.
- Can write accounts of experiences, describing feelings and reactions in simple connected text.
- Can give very short basic descriptions of past activities and personal experiences.
- Can tell a simple story (e.g. about a holiday or life in the future).
- Can narrate a story.
- Can write personal emails/letters asking for or conveying simple information.
- Can write personal letters giving news and expressing thoughts about abstract/cultural topics (music, films).
- Can write personal letters describing experiences, feelings and events in some detail.
- Can write notes conveying simple information to friends.
- Can take a short, simple message provided they can ask for repetition.

MEDIATION
- Can play a supportive role in interaction provided others speak slowly and help.
- Can convey relevant information from clearly structured, short, simple, informational texts on concrete, familiar subjects in simple everyday language.

LINGUISTIC COMPETENCE
- Vocabulary: Sufficient for routine, everyday transactions in familiar situations.
- Grammar: Uses reasonably accurately frequently used routines and patterns; makes basic mistakes but communicative intent is clear.
- Vocabulary control: Good control of elementary vocabulary; major errors on complex thoughts or unfamiliar topics.
- Phonology: Generally clear enough to be understood despite noticeable foreign accent; partners may need to ask for repetition.
- Orthography: Spelling, punctuation and layout accurate enough to be followed most of the time.
- Sociolinguistic: Can perform and respond to basic language functions (information exchange, requests); can express opinions and attitudes simply.

CLASS: C GYMNASIUM | CEFR LEVEL: B1
================================================================

LISTENING
- Can understand straightforward factual information on common topics, identifying both general messages and specific details.
- Can understand the main points of clear standard speech on familiar matters including short narratives.
- Can understand the information content of majority of recorded/broadcast audio on personal interest topics.
- Can understand main points of radio news bulletins and simpler recorded material.
- Can follow many films and TV programmes where visuals and action carry much of the storyline.

READING
- Can read straightforward factual texts on subjects related to interests with satisfactory comprehension.
- Can understand main points of clear standard input on familiar matters.
- Can understand basic types of standard routine letters/emails on familiar topics.
- Can scan longer texts to locate desired information; gather information from different parts of a text.
- Can recognise the line of argument; can identify main conclusions in clearly signalled argumentative texts.

WRITING
- Can write straightforward connected texts on a range of familiar subjects, linking shorter elements into a linear sequence.
- Can convey information and ideas on abstract and concrete topics with reasonable precision.
- Can write notes conveying simple information to friends, teachers, service people.
- Can write personal letters/emails describing experiences, feelings and events in some detail.
- Can write personal letters/emails giving news and expressing thoughts about abstract/cultural topics.
- Can write straightforward detailed descriptions on familiar subjects.
- Can write a description of an event, a recent trip (real or imagined).
- Can narrate a story.
- Can write short, simple essays on topics of interest.

SPEAKING
- Can enter unprepared into conversation on familiar topics; express personal opinions; exchange information on familiar topics.
- Can express thoughts on more abstract, cultural topics (films, books, music).
- Can reasonably fluently sustain a straightforward description, presenting it as a linear sequence of points.
- Can keep going comprehensibly, even though pausing for grammatical and lexical planning is evident.
- Can maintain a conversation but may sometimes be difficult to follow.
- Can follow much of what is said on general topics; give or seek personal views.
- Can express belief, opinion, agreement and disagreement politely.
- Can relate the plot of a book or film and describe reactions.
- Can give detailed accounts of experiences, describing feelings and reactions.
- Can express opinions on everyday subjects; briefly give reasons and explanations; explain whether they approve of something.

MEDIATION
- Can collaborate with people from other backgrounds showing interest and empathy.
- Can convey main points made in long texts in uncomplicated language on topics of personal interest.
- Can summarise (in Language B) main points from clear, well-structured texts (in Language A) on familiar subjects.
- Can summarise (in Language B) main points from a conversation (in Language A) on a subject of personal interest.
- Can summarise in writing (in Language B) main points from informational texts (in Language A).

LINGUISTIC COMPETENCE
- Vocabulary: Sufficient to express with some circumlocutions on most everyday topics (family, hobbies, work, travel, current events).
- Grammar: Reasonable accuracy in familiar contexts; good control with noticeable mother tongue influence; errors occur but intent is clear.
- Vocabulary control: Good control of elementary vocabulary; major errors on complex thoughts or unfamiliar topics.
- Phonology: Clearly intelligible even if foreign accent sometimes evident and occasional mispronunciations occur.
- Orthography: Continuous writing generally intelligible; spelling, punctuation and layout accurate enough most of the time.
- Sociolinguistic: Can perform and respond to a wide range of language functions using most common exponents in neutral register.

CLASS: B LYCEUM | CEFR LEVEL: B1+
================================================================

LISTENING
- Can understand straightforward factual information on common topics, identifying general messages and specific details.
- Can understand standard spoken language, live or broadcast, on familiar and unfamiliar topics in personal, social, academic or vocational life.
- Can follow extended speech and complex lines of argument provided topic is familiar and talk is sign-posted.
- Can understand information content of majority of recorded/broadcast audio on personal interest topics.
- Can understand main points of radio news bulletins and recorded material on familiar subjects.
- Can understand most radio documentaries and other recorded/broadcast audio in standard dialect; can identify speaker's mood and tone.
- Can understand a large part of many TV programmes on personal interest topics.

READING
- Can read straightforward factual texts with satisfactory comprehension.
- Can read with a large degree of independence, adapting style and speed to different texts.
- Can understand standard routine letters/emails on familiar topics; find relevant information in everyday material.
- Can scan longer texts to locate desired information.
- Can recognise the line of argument; identify main conclusions in argumentative texts.
- Can understand articles and reports on contemporary problems.

WRITING
- Can write straightforward connected texts on familiar subjects, linking shorter elements into a linear sequence.
- Can write clear, detailed texts on a variety of subjects.
- Can convey information and ideas on abstract and concrete topics with reasonable precision.
- Can express news and views effectively in writing.
- Can write personal emails describing experiences, feelings, events or giving news and expressing thoughts.
- Can write notes conveying simple information to friends, teachers, service people.
- Can write clear, detailed descriptions on a variety of subjects.
- Can narrate a story.
- Can write short, simple essays on topics of interest.
- Can write an essay developing an argument, giving reasons for/against a point of view, explaining advantages and disadvantages.
- Can summarise, report and give opinion on accumulated factual information.

SPEAKING
- Can enter unprepared into conversation on familiar topics; express personal opinions; exchange information.
- Can express thoughts on more abstract, cultural topics.
- Can keep going comprehensibly, with pausing for grammatical/lexical planning evident.
- Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity making sustained relationships with native speakers possible.
- Can convey degrees of emotion; highlight personal significance of events and experiences.
- Can follow much of what is said; give or seek personal views.
- Can express belief, opinion, agreement and disagreement politely.
- Can account for and sustain opinions by providing relevant explanations, arguments and comments.
- Can take an active part in informal discussion, evaluate alternative proposals, make and respond to hypotheses.
- Can outline an issue clearly, speculate about causes/consequences, weigh advantages/disadvantages.
- Can pass on detailed information reliably; can explain a problem requiring the service provider to make a concession.
- Can take initiatives in an interview; expand and develop ideas.
- Can develop an argument well enough to be followed without difficulty most of the time.

MEDIATION
- Can summarise (in Language B) main points from long texts (in Language A) on topics in their fields of interest.
- Can summarise (in Language B) a short narrative, article, talk, discussion, interview or documentary.
- Can collate short pieces of information from several sources (in Language A) and summarise (in Language B).
- Can summarise in writing (in Language B) information and arguments from texts (in Language A).

LINGUISTIC COMPETENCE
- Vocabulary: Sufficient to express with some circumlocutions on most everyday topics.
- Grammar: Reasonable accuracy in familiar contexts; relatively high degree of grammatical control; does not make mistakes leading to misunderstanding.
- Vocabulary control: Lexical accuracy generally high; some confusion and incorrect word choice without hindering communication.
- Phonology: Clearly intelligible even if foreign accent sometimes evident.
- Orthography: Continuous writing generally intelligible; spelling and punctuation reasonably accurate.
- Sociolinguistic: Can express appropriately in situations; can with some effort keep up with and contribute to group discussions.

Note: B Lyceum Evening Schools share the same B1+ descriptors.

CLASS: C LYCEUM | CEFR LEVEL: B2
================================================================

LISTENING
- Can understand standard spoken language, live or broadcast, on familiar and unfamiliar topics in personal, social, academic or vocational life. Only extreme background noise, inadequate discourse structure or idiomatic usage may impede understanding.
- Can understand main ideas of propositionally and linguistically complex speech on concrete and abstract topics in standard dialect, including technical discussions in field of specialisation.
- Can follow essentials of lectures, talks, reports and other academic/professional presentations.
- Can understand announcements and messages on concrete and abstract topics in standard dialect at normal speed.
- Can understand recordings in standard dialect; identify speaker viewpoints and attitudes as well as information content.
- Can understand most radio documentaries and other recorded/broadcast audio; identify speaker's mood, tone.
- Can understand most TV news and current affairs programmes.
- Can understand documentaries, live interviews, talk shows, plays and majority of films in standard dialect.

READING
- Can read with a large degree of independence, adapting style and speed to different texts and purposes, using appropriate reference sources selectively. Broad active reading vocabulary; may have difficulty with low-frequency idioms.
- Can understand in detail lengthy, complex texts, provided can reread difficult sections.
- Can read correspondence in field of interest and readily grasp the essential meaning.
- Can scan quickly through long and complex texts, locating relevant details.
- Can quickly identify content and relevance of news items, articles and reports.
- Can understand articles and reports on contemporary problems.
- Can understand specialised articles outside field with occasional dictionary use.
- Can obtain information, ideas and opinions from highly specialised sources.
- Can understand lengthy, complex instructions, including conditions and warnings.

WRITING
- Can write clear, detailed texts on a variety of subjects, synthesising and evaluating information and arguments from a number of sources.
- Can write clear, well-structured texts on complex subjects, expanding and supporting points of view, rounding off with an appropriate conclusion.
- Can convey information and ideas on abstract and concrete topics with reasonable precision.
- Can express news and views effectively in writing.
- Can write letters/emails conveying degrees of emotion, highlighting personal significance of events and experiences.
- Can write clear, detailed descriptions of real or imaginary events marking relationships between ideas, following genre conventions.
- Can write a review of a film, book or play.
- Can write an essay or report developing an argument, giving reasons for/against a point of view, explaining advantages and disadvantages.
- Can evaluate different ideas or solutions to a problem.
- Can write an essay/report developing an argument systematically with appropriate highlighting of significant points.

SPEAKING
- Can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity making regular interaction with native speakers possible without strain.
- Can use language fluently, accurately and effectively on a wide range of general, academic, vocational or leisure topics, marking clearly the relationships between ideas.
- Can communicate spontaneously with good grammatical control without having to restrict what is said; adopts appropriate level of formality.
- Can convey degrees of emotion; highlight personal significance of events and experiences.
- Can engage in extended conversation on most general topics in a clearly participatory fashion.
- Can take an active part in informal discussion; comment, put point of view clearly, evaluate alternative proposals, make and respond to hypotheses.
- Can express ideas and opinions with precision; present and respond to complex lines of argument convincingly.
- Can keep up with an animated discussion between native speakers; accurately identify arguments.
- Can outline an issue or problem clearly, speculate about causes/consequences, weigh advantages/disadvantages.
- Can understand detailed instructions reliably.
- Can synthesise and report information and arguments from a number of sources.
- Can take initiatives in an interview; expand and develop ideas with little prompting.
- Can carry out effective, fluent interview, departing spontaneously from prepared questions.
- Can give clear, detailed descriptions and presentations, expanding and supporting ideas with subsidiary points and examples.
- Can develop a clear argument, expanding and supporting points of view at length.
- Can construct a chain of reasoned argument.
- Can explain a viewpoint on a topical issue giving advantages and disadvantages.

MEDIATION
- Can work collaboratively with people from different backgrounds, creating a positive atmosphere, identifying common goals, comparing options, explaining suggestions.
- Can further develop others' ideas, pose questions that invite reactions, propose a solution or next steps.
- Can convey detailed information and arguments reliably from complex well-structured texts.
- Can synthesise and report (in Language B) information and arguments from a number of sources (in Language A).
- Can summarise (in Language B) important points from longer, complex texts (in Language A) on subjects of current interest.
- Can summarise (in Language B) the plot and sequence of events in a film or play.
- Can summarise in writing (in Language B) main content of complex texts (in Language A).
- Can take notes from clearly structured lectures on familiar subjects.
- Can summarise a wide range of factual and imaginative texts, commenting on contrasting points of view and main themes.

LINGUISTIC COMPETENCE
- Vocabulary: Good range for matters connected to field and most general topics; can vary formulation to avoid repetition; lexical gaps can still cause hesitation.
- Grammar: Relatively high degree of grammatical control; does not make mistakes leading to misunderstanding. Occasional slips or non-systematic errors may occur but are rare.
- Vocabulary control: Lexical accuracy generally high; some confusion and incorrect word choice without hindering communication.
- Phonology: Clear, natural pronunciation and intonation.
- Orthography: Continuous writing generally intelligible; spelling and punctuation reasonably accurate; may show signs of mother tongue influence.
- Sociolinguistic: Can express confidently, clearly and politely in formal or informal register appropriate to situation. Can with some effort keep up with and contribute to group discussions even when speech is fast and colloquial.

Note: C Lyceum Evening Schools share the same B2 descriptors.

CLASS: A TECHNICAL SCHOOL (ΤΕΣΕΚ) | CEFR LEVEL: A2+
================================================================
Same descriptors as B Gymnasium (A2+). See B Gymnasium entry above.

Key differences in context: Vocational orientation.
- Vocabulary topics include job-related language, workplace signs and instructions.
- Reading for orientation includes understanding safety regulations.
- Speaking includes discussing employment situations.

- Writing tasks include describing work experiences.

================================================================
HOW THE AGENT SHOULD USE THESE DESCRIPTORS
================================================================

1. When generating activities, always check the CEFR level for the
   selected class using the Quick Reference table above.

2. Use the "Can do" statements to pitch tasks correctly:
   - Support tier tasks should target descriptors at the lower end
     or from the class below.
   - Core tier tasks should target the descriptors for the selected class.
   - Challenge tier tasks should target descriptors at the upper end
     or from the class above.

3. When referencing curriculum alignment in output, cite the specific
   skill area descriptor. Example:
   "This task targets the B1 Writing descriptor: Can write straightforward
   connected texts on a range of familiar subjects within the field of
   interest."

4. For Mediation tasks, always refer to the Mediation descriptors for
   the selected class — these are specific to the Cyprus curriculum
   and not covered by generic CEFR frameworks.

5. For Evening School classes, note that some have lower CEFR targets
   than their day school equivalents (e.g. B Gymnasium Evening = A2,
   not A2+). Always check the Evening Schools summary above.
